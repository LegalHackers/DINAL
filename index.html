Welcome to Do I Need a License?

Click Here to Get Started

We are going to ask you a few questions about the work you want to use and what you want to do with it. In each question, check all answers that apply.

After you answer the questions, we will give you a (very) broad recommendation on whether you need a license to use that work, based on a developing algorithm that considers US copyright law.

NOTE THAT IT'S ALWAYS SAFEST TO ASK FOR CONSENT BEFORE KNOWINGLY USING SOMEONE ELSE'S WORK.

Got It

Before we get started, there are certain things that cannot have US copyright, so they are free to use under US copyright law (only):

  • a work made by the US federal government
  • a work in the public domain
  • an unwritten idea or concept
  • a work you are copying for a non-profit archive or library
